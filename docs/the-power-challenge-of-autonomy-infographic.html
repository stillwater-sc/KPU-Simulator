The Power Challenge

Why the Future of Autonomy Depends on Deep Silicon Innovation

Part 1: The Wall of Infeasibility

To power a Level 4 autonomous vehicle, the compute demand is immense. If we use today's general-purpose CPUs, the power required is astronomical—far beyond what any vehicle can support. This isn't just an engineering challenge; it's a fundamental physical barrier.

The chart above uses a logarithmic scale. The typical high-performance multi-core CPU solution would require over 8.3 million watts, demonstrating its impracticality as our power budget is just 1000 watts.

Part 2: The Limits of Current AI Accelerators

Specialized AI accelerators from the data center offer a massive leap in efficiency. However, when applied to an in-vehicle power budget of 1,000 watts, even these powerful chips fall short, consuming far too much energy for practical deployment.

Part 3: The Path to Feasibility

The solution lies in ultra-efficient, purpose-built silicon. By designing chips specifically for edge AI tasks, it becomes possible to meet the demanding compute requirements of various autonomous systems—from warehouse robots to fully autonomous cars—all while staying comfortably within a practical power budget.
